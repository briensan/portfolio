Cross-Industry Financial Benchmarking

Comparing efficiency, scale, and profitability across Pharmaceuticals, Automotive, and Tech Hardware using public financial data.

CompaniesPfizer, Ford, Apple
TypeGroup project
FocusMargin, ROA, inventory turnover

Objective

The brief was to analyze three companies from three different industries — Pfizer (Pharmaceuticals), Ford (Automobile), and Apple (Tech Hardware) — and compare their performance using public financial data, rather than assuming that a larger company is automatically a stronger one.

Metrics used

Four metrics anchored the comparison, each answering a different question about performance:

Gross Margin Net Margin Return on Assets (ROA) Inventory Turnover

Sector comparison

SectorInventoryProfile
Pharmaceuticals (Pfizer)ModerateStable profits, balanced overall
Automotive (Ford)HighLeads on revenue and assets; large scale, higher cost
Tech Hardware (Apple)LowVery high profit; best profitability and efficiency

The pattern that emerged: auto wins on scale, tech wins on profitability, and pharma sits in between — stable but not standout on either axis.

Zooming into pharma: Pfizer vs. competitors

CompanyPosition
PfizerLarge asset base, but lower profit relative to size
Eli LillyBest efficiency of the three
AbbVieHigh debt load

This comparison mattered because it showed that within a single sector, size and financial health don't move together — Pfizer's scale didn't translate into the best margins, and AbbVie's results came with more balance-sheet risk.

Industry trend: the COVID shock

All three industries saw a drop in 2020, followed by a strong recovery — with tech growing fastest out of the three.

Takeaway

Efficiency and cost control drive success

Tech led on profitability, auto dominated on scale, and pharma held a competitive, balanced position. The broader point for financial analysis: revenue and asset size tell you how big a company is, but margin and turnover ratios tell you how well it's actually running — and the two rankings don't have to agree.
